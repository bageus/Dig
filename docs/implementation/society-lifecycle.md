# Society, families and resident lifecycle

## State ownership

`SocietyState` is the authoritative owner of relationships, partnerships, parent links, pregnancies and the resident family graph. It does not own needs, movement, jobs, inventory or presentation state.

The surrounding ownership remains explicit:

- Agents owns health, active actions and the living simulation actor;
- Society owns social identity, sex, family links and reproduction eligibility;
- Lifecycle rules inside Society map age ticks to Child, Adult, Old and Deceased stages;
- World/Agents supply the last known logical position;
- Application coordinates cleanup in Jobs and Inventory after a typed death event;
- Presentation consumes immutable snapshots and typed events only.

## Identity and names

`ResidentNameCatalog` contains separate ordered female and male name lists. Empty or duplicate names are rejected. `ResidentIdentityGenerator` derives sex, name, deterministic `EntityId`, potential and inherited traits from:

```text
WorldSeed + birth sequence + named random streams
```

The generator never uses Unity random, frame order or localized display strings as identity keys.

Trait inheritance is data-driven through `TraitInheritanceDefinition`. Each trait defines separate chances for one-parent and both-parent inheritance. Potential is based on the parent average plus a bounded deterministic variance.

## Relationships and family graph

Social bonds store sympathy, trust and the last interaction tick for a canonical resident pair. Partnership creation requires:

- two living adults;
- no existing different partner;
- configured sympathy and trust thresholds;
- no close relationship within the configured ancestor depth.

Parent links are immutable after birth. `ValidateFamilyGraph` rejects ancestor cycles. Close-kin checks cover direct ancestors and shared ancestors within the configured depth.

## Reproduction diagnostics

`EvaluateReproduction` returns an ordered typed reason list. It checks:

- registered and living residents;
- female mother and male father for the current binary content contract;
- Adult stage;
- mutual partnership;
- close kinship;
- existing pregnancy;
- Mood and Health thresholds;
- fertility modifier;
- available birth place.

The default design boundary is represented by data: Mood `7500` is neutral and fails a `7600` minimum, while Mood `7600` satisfies only the mood condition. It never bypasses the remaining Society rules.

## Pregnancy, birth and lifecycle

Pregnancy records father, conception tick and due tick. Birth is accepted only for the matching due pregnancy and creates one child with immutable parent links.

`AdvanceLifecycle` deterministically derives stages from birth tick and policy thresholds. Every transition is applied once. Reaching maximum age records an `old_age` death.

## Typed events

Society publishes:

- `ResidentBorn` with stable event id, identity, sex, parents and position;
- `ResidentLifeStageChanged` with previous/new stage;
- `ResidentDied` with cause and last known logical position;
- partnership and pregnancy facts for Application/Presentation consumers.

Birth, stage and death event ids are stable and are not generated by HUD polling.

## Death and cleanup contract

`RecordDeath` is idempotent. It stores the last position, clears pregnancy and partnership state and publishes one `ResidentDied` fact.

`AgentDeathLifecycleHandler` converts the existing Agents-owned `AgentDied` fact into the Society lifecycle record. It reads the authoritative `AgentSnapshot`, rejects a still-living actor, uses the logical position from that snapshot and records the typed cause `health_depleted`.

`ResidentDeathCleanupHandler` consumes any `ResidentDied` event, including old age or external causes. It deterministically:

1. finds non-terminal Jobs assigned to the resident;
2. cancels them with reason `resident_died`;
3. releases worker, position, designation and other Job reservations through `JobSystem`;
4. releases Inventory quantity reservations for the same job ids;
5. publishes the resulting Job and Inventory events.

Replaying the same death event is a no-op because cancelled jobs no longer have an assigned resident and all related reservations have already been released. The immutable family record remains available after removal from active simulation membership.

## Validation

Tests cover:

- the exact 75/76 mood boundary;
- due pregnancy and one birth event;
- sibling kinship blocking;
- Child/Adult/Old/Deceased transitions exactly once;
- idempotent death and last-position preservation;
- deterministic identity and inheritance;
- a multi-generation acyclic family graph;
- cancellation of only the dead resident's jobs;
- Job and Inventory reservation cleanup;
- repeated cleanup without duplicate events;
- `AgentDied` synchronization with Society cause and position.
